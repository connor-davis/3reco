# Business Users

Business users are the buyers on the 3rEco platform. They source recyclable materials from collectors (C2B transactions) and can also trade with other businesses (B2B transactions).

## Onboarding

After sign-up, a business user completes their profile:

- Business name
- Business registration number
- Contact details (phone, address, city, province, area code)
- Acceptance of terms

Until `profileComplete` is `true`, the user is redirected to the profile completion form.

## Business Dashboard

The business dashboard shows spending and procurement analytics:

| Stat | Calculation |
|---|---|
| **Total Spend** | Sum of `price × weight` for all purchases |
| **Total Volume** | Sum of `weight` for all purchases (kg) |
| **Carbon Savings** | Sum of `weight × material.carbonFactor` |
| **Transaction Count** | Number of completed transactions as buyer |

All stats support **date range filtering** so businesses can view monthly or quarterly reports.

## Marketplace Workflow

### Sending a Transaction Request (C2B)

1. Browse the marketplace — stock listed by collectors appears here
2. Click a stock item to view details (material, weight, price per kg)
3. Send a **transaction request** — this creates a `transactionRequest` with `status: "pending"`
4. The collector receives a real-time notification
5. The collector responds with an **offer** (may adjust weight and price)
6. The business can **accept** or **reject** the offer
7. On acceptance, a `transaction` record is created and an invoice PDF is generated

### Sending a Transaction Request (B2B)

The flow is identical to C2B, but both parties are `type: "business"`. B2B transactions are tagged with `type: "b2b"` in the `transactions` table.

## Stock Listings

Business users can also list their own stock for B2B sales:

```
Business lists surplus stock (isListed: true)
       ↓
Another business finds it in marketplace
       ↓
Sends transaction request
       ↓
Negotiation → acceptance → B2B transaction
```

## Negotiation Messages

During an active transaction request, both parties can exchange messages:

```typescript
await convex.mutation(api.transactionRequestMessages.send, {
  transactionId: requestId,
  content: 'Can you do 500kg at R8.50/kg?',
})
```

Messages appear in real time in the negotiation thread and are stored against the `transactionRequests` document.

## Transaction History

Businesses can view all their completed transactions, filtered by:
- Date range
- Transaction type (C2B / B2B)
- Material

Each transaction links to the auto-generated **PDF invoice** stored in Convex file storage.
